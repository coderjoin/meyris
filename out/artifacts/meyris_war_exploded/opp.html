OPPORTUNITIES

  • China landscape

    • China’s GDP grew by 6.8% and 6.7% in the first and second quarter of 2018 respectively. In the third quarter of 2018, GDP grew by 6.5%.

    China’s B2C e-commerce sales are set to surpass 956 billion U.S. dollars in revenues in 2022, up from 403 billion U.S. dollars in 2016.

    It can be said, that China’s market is one of the largest and most dynamic in the world, but it can also be extremely complicated and expensive to navigate, especially for foreign brands.

    It is important for players to prepare well for this complex market, and more importantly, to play by the rules. Resources and channels also play an essential role towards success.

    With years of experience in the industry, MEYRIS has experience with China’s digital landscape as well as deep insights generated by our senior experts. By providing step by step strategic advice, we help to lower the entry barrier and make the business much easier for non-natives.

  • KOLs

    • Key Opinion Leaders are an essential asset for any Chinese social media marketing strategy. KOLs are online celebrities with millions of fans on various forms of social media. KOLs leverage their huge followership to promote brands and engage with different products. One such example of a KOL would be Becky Li, who sold 100 Mini Cooper Countryman cars within five minutes on her blog.

  • Daigou

    • A Daigou is a channel of commerce in which a person outside of China purchases commodities for a customer in mainland China.

    Approximately 80% of Chinese luxury purchases are made abroad. The Daigou market across sectors amounts to 15 billion USD annually. Daigous are not only personal shoppers, but also social media influencers. Daigous buy luxury goods privately, to pass on to their customers. A 2015 survey of Chinese online luxury shoppers found that 35% have used Daigou to purchase luxury goods online, while only 7% used the website of the brand they are buying, or think they are buying.
